First of all, we'd like to thank Bryant (this guy) who gave us the inspiration and idea to create this part of the website by claiming that Himawari shows proof of the globe because it has (almost) live images of clouds. That's great. We can have cloud data for a flat earth too; simply click on the cloud icon. It might be ugly, but that's due to the compression of the entire earth's surface into the few inches on your screen and we will not alter our data to make it look better. We're not in the business of faking things. That's what NASA does. The clouds image isn't live, but not older than 6 hours either. We'll work to updating the clouds at least every hour in the near future.

The site is simple to use and understand. By default it shows the real, live position of the sun and the moon and day and night. Click on the icons on the right side of your screen to see what they do or continue reading.

Clicking on the icon for the sun, moon or clouds will either show or hide the sun, moon and clouds. Click on the play button for an animated view of the next 24 hours days and night cycle. Clicking it multiple times will not advance further into the future. Only the next 24 hours starting from now will be played.

Data

Our wind data is updated every 6 hours following the releases of the US National Weather Service. The magnetic flux data is provided by NOAA and updated daily. The Ocean currents are updated approximately every 5 days.

About

This site is based on the open source code of earth.nullschool.net and was built as a replacement after they, i.e. nullschool.net, decided to remove the Azimuthal Equidistant projection under the pretense of it being bugged. This site proves it is not. We offer the same AE (Azimuthal Equidistant) projection, as well as a modified version of the Conic Equidistant Map. Although we cannot prove these projections to be 100% correct, it is our personal belief they provide a fairly accurate depiction of the Earth's layout.
